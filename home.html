    Health Help

    "You can't see the air, but you sure can feel it when it's not right"

    Air is a mix of gases, mainly nitrogen (78%) and oxygen (21%), vital for life on Earth. Air quality measures how clean or polluted the air is, impacting health and the environment.
    Good air quality means low pollution, supporting healthy living and ecosystems, while poor air quality can cause respiratory and cardiovascular issues. Pollution comes from sources like vehicles, industries, and natural events. Air quality indices (AQIs) help monitor pollution levels.
    Improving air quality involves using cleaner energy, enforcing emission standards, and adopting sustainable practices. Understanding and addressing air quality helps protect public health and the environment.
